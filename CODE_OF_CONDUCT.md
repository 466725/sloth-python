# Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

We are committed to providing a welcoming, inclusive, and respectful community for everyone, regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to creating a positive environment include:

- **Being respectful** - Treat all community members with kindness and respect
- **Being inclusive** - Welcome diverse perspectives and backgrounds
- **Being constructive** - Provide helpful, thoughtful feedback
- **Being patient** - Understand that learning takes time
- **Being professional** - Keep communications focused and on-topic
- **Listening actively** - Seek to understand before being understood
- **Giving credit** - Acknowledge others' contributions and ideas

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

- Harassment, discrimination, or bullying of any kind
- Offensive comments related to personal characteristics
- Unwelcome sexual attention or advances
- Threats of violence or violent language
- Deliberate intimidation or stalking
- Publishing others' private information without consent
- Other conduct considered inappropriate in a professional setting
- Spam, advertising, or self-promotion without relevance

## Enforcement

Community leaders are responsible for clarifying and enforcing this Code of Conduct. All violations will be reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly.

### Reporting

If you experience or witness behavior that violates this Code of Conduct:

1. **Do not engage directly** - Avoid escalating the situation
2. **Document the incident** - Note what happened, when, and who was involved
3. **Report privately** - Contact maintainers via:
   - Direct message to a maintainer
   - Email to project maintainers (if provided)
   - Report through GitHub if available

All reports are treated confidentially and seriously.

### Consequences

Violations may result in:
- **Warning** - For minor first-time violations
- **Temporary muting** - Preventing participation in discussions
- **Removal** - From the community, project, or organization

The severity of consequences depends on the nature and context of the violation.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to:
- All project spaces (GitHub, Discussions, Issues)
- Project-related spaces and events
- Official communications on behalf of the project
